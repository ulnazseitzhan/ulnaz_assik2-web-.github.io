Hayao Miyazaki

About Miyazaki

Hayao Miyazaki (born 1941) is a Japanese animator, filmmaker, and co-founder of Studio Ghibli. He is known for his beautifully crafted films that blend fantasy, environmental themes, and strong characters. His work has inspired generations of animators and audiences worldwide.

Life Events

  1. 1941 – Born in Tokyo, Japan
  2. 1963 – Began career at Toei Animation
  3. 1985 – Co-founded Studio Ghibli
  4. 2001 – Released "Spirited Away" (won Academy Award)
  5. 2023 – Released "The Boy and the Heron"

Key Works

  • My Neighbor Totoro (1988)
  • Kiki's Delivery Service (1989)
  • Princess Mononoke (1997)
  • Spirited Away (2001)
  • The Wind Rises (2013)
